alignmentUtils.orio_simmx¶

alignmentUtils.orio_simmx(M, D)[source]¶

Calculate an Orio&Schwartz-style (Peak Structure Distance) similarity matrix. M is the binary mask (where each column corresponds to a row in the output matrix S); D is the regular spectrogram, where columns of S correspond to columns of D (spectrogram time slices). Each value is the proportion of energy going through the mask to the total energy of the column of D, thus lies between 0 (no match) and 1 (mask completely covers energy in D column).
